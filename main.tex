\begin{jabstract}\setlength{\parindent}{1zw}%
\hspace{1zw}本プロジェクトでは，公立はこだて未来大学（以下，未来大学）を発着する路線バスにおいて，一部の便に利用者が集中し，車内の混雑や乗車できない利用者の発生，運行の遅延につながる可能性があることに着目した．そこで，数理モデリングを用いて，その混雑を緩和する運行方法の提案を目指す．また，運転手不足や運行コストなどの制約があるため，単純な増便だけに頼らず，既存の車両や運転手を活用しながら輸送効率を向上させる方法を検討する．

前期には，数理モデリングや数値計算法に関する基礎知識を身につけるため，書籍を用いた輪講会を実施した．その後，未来大学を発着するバスの混雑を緩和するための複数の解決策を比較した．その結果，一部の便を快速化し，短縮された時間を利用して亀田支所前からはこだて未来大学までの区間を同一車両で再度運行する案を，今後の検証対象として選定した．また，乗降者数の計測や時刻表に基づく運行状況の可視化を行い，今後の検証に向けた予備的な調査を進めた．

現時点では，具体的な数理モデルの構築や導入効果の検証には至っていない．今後は，バスの運行や利用状況に関するデータを収集し，現在の運行状況を表現する数理モデルを構築する．そのうえで，複数の運行パターンを比較し，混雑や遅延，利用者およびバス事業者への影響を定量的に評価することで，実現可能な運行方法の提示を目指す．
\bunseki{佐々木出}

\begin{eabstract}\setlength{\parindent}{1zw}%
\hspace{1zw}This project aims to propose feasible bus operation methods that mitigate congestion on the route buses serving Future University Hakodate by means of mathematical modeling. During commuting hours, passengers tend to concentrate on particular buses, which may lead to crowded vehicles, passengers unable to board the buses they wish to take, and delays in operation. Since constraints such as a shortage of drivers and operating costs make it unrealistic to rely solely on increasing the number of buses, we examined ways to improve transport efficiency while utilizing the existing vehicles and drivers.

In the first semester, we held reading seminars using textbooks to acquire fundamental knowledge of mathematical modeling and numerical methods. We then compared several possible solutions for mitigating the congestion of the buses serving the university. As a result, we selected a plan in which some buses are operated as rapid services, and the time saved is used to operate the same vehicle again on the section between the Kameda-shisho-mae stop and the university, as the subject of further verification. In addition, we conducted preliminary investigations, such as counting the number of passengers boarding and alighting, and visualizing bus operations based on timetables.

At present, we have not yet constructed a concrete mathematical model or verified the effectiveness of the proposed method. In the future, we will collect data on bus operations and usage, construct a mathematical model that reproduces the current situation, and compare multiple operation patterns through simulation. By quantitatively evaluating congestion, delays, and the effects on passengers and the bus operator, we aim to propose feasible bus operation methods.
\bunseki{佐々木出}
